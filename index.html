05

History &
Culture

Harbin, a pearl embedded along the Songhua River, carries a century of historical vicissitudes and cultural fusion. In 1898, the construction of the Chinese Eastern Railway transformed this small fishing village into an international metropolis, where immigrants from over 20 countries including Russia, Jewish, and Poland gathered, leaving behind architectural treasures such as Saint Sophia Cathedral and Central Street. This is one of the earliest Chinese cities to embrace European civilization, and also China's Music City, birthplace of the nation's first symphony orchestra. From Jin Dynasty culture to ice and snow culture, from industrial powerhouse to tourism destination, Harbin tells the legend and dreams of a city with its unique multicultural charm.
